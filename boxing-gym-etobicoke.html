West End Athletic Club is a full boxing and fitness gym at 283 Bering Ave, in the heart of Toronto's west end. Beginner to competitor, ages 4 to 64 — the energy of family, the standards of champions.

The west end's home for boxing.

Whatever brought you here — fitness, skill, stress relief, or the fight game — there's a place for you on our floor.

Tucked into Etobicoke at 283 Bering Ave — just off The Queensway near Islington — West End Athletic Club is where Toronto's west end learns to box. We welcome complete beginners, busy professionals, parents, youth athletes, and competitors under one roof, with certified coaches who include national champions and a Team Canada boxer. No experience required, no ego at the door, and no sparring unless you want it.

Members come from across the west end — Mimico, New Toronto, The Junction, Bloor West Village, High Park and Roncesvalles — for real coaching in a community that feels like family. Rated 4.9 stars across 117 reviews, with free on-site parking, a full ring, heavy-bag floor, strength area, and sauna. It's boxing done right.

Common questions.

Everything you need to know before your first visit to the gym.

Where are you located?

We're at 283 Bering Ave in Etobicoke, in Toronto's west end — just off The Queensway near Islington, minutes from Mimico, New Toronto, The Junction, Bloor West Village and High Park, with free on-site parking.

Do I need any boxing experience?

Not at all. Most of our members started with little or no boxing experience. Our coaches teach the fundamentals step-by-step in a supportive, welcoming environment, and no sparring is ever required.

Is there parking?

Yes. We offer free on-site parking at 283 Bering Ave, with additional street parking nearby.

What classes do you offer?

Beginner boxing (The Boxing Blueprint), fitness boxing (Tech-Fit), boxing bootcamp, bag work, strength & conditioning (Build & Burn), women's-only classes, youth boxing for ages 4–16, beginner sparring, and an amateur fight team.

Can I try the gym before joining?

Yes. New members can train for three consecutive days free — walk every floor, try the classes, and meet the coaches before committing to a membership.

What are your hours?

Monday to Thursday 6am–9pm, Friday 6am–8:30pm, Saturday 8am–3pm, and Sunday 9am–2pm.
